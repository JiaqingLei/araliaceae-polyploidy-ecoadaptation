# araliaceae-polyploidy-ecoadaptation
Code and protocol for analyzing genomic and transcriptomic remodeling associated with polyploidy and ecological adaptation in Araliaceae.

> **Associated Manuscript:** *Divergent genome fractionation and adaptation following polyploidy in the Araliaceae*

## 📌 Project Overview

Polyploidization and subsequent diploidization (WGD/post-polyploidy reshuffling) are fundamental driving forces in eukaryotic evolution. This study investigates whether ancestral genome reshuffling and biased genetic fractionation following polyploidy cycles are associated with genomic variations that drove the phenotypic and ecological diversification in the **Araliaceae** family.

By integrating **20 genomes/subgenomes** and **561 transcriptomes** across species subjected to simulated open-canopy and shaded-understory light regimes, this repository provides the full suite of bioinformatics pipelines, custom scripts, and analytical protocols used to link genome architecture to ecological adaptation.

```
[Genomic Data (20 Genomes/Subgenomes) + Simulated-Habitat Transcriptomes (561 Samples)]
       │
       ├──► 1. Comparative Genomics & Subgenome Fractionation (Synteny, WGD, & Fractionation Bias)
       ├──► 2. Pan-Genome & Transcriptomic Plasticity (five Light Regimes across Herbs, Shrubs, & Trees)
       └──► 3. Regulationary Network Reshaping (Photomorphogenesis & Photosynthesis Pathway Divergence)
       │
[Insights into Post-Polyploidy Reshuffling & Ecological Adaptation]
```

## 🔬 Key Analytical Pipelines Included

This project is organized into three core analytical modules:

### 1. Comparative Genomics & Subgenome Fractionation

Polyploidy event identification, syntenic block detection, and genome-wide synteny analysis.

### 2. Pan-Genome & Simulated-Habitat Transcriptomic Profiling

Comparative evaluation of phenotypic plasticity and gene expression dynamics across different growth forms (herbs, shrubs, and trees).

### 3. Targeted Regulatory & Pathway Remodeling

Network analysis focusing on genetic fractionation and transcriptional remodeling within photomorphogenesis and photosynthesis/carbon fixation pathways.
